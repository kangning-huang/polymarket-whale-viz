# Website Branding Options

## Context

We're building a platform where visitors watch Polymarket trading bots compete in real-time crypto prediction markets. The audience is curious speculators who want to:
1. See which bots are winning and what moves they make
2. Decipher bot strategies to gain real profits themselves

The current concept: **"polybot-arena.com"** — an arena where polybots compete.

Below are 10 naming options with pros/cons analysis.

---

## 1. **polybot-arena.com**

**Pros:**
- Clear concept: "arena" = competition, spectacle, entertainment
- "polybot" is a catchy portmanteau (Polymarket + bot)
- Implies multiple bots fighting for dominance
- Gaming/esports energy appeals to crypto natives

**Cons:**
- "Arena" might feel too gamified for serious traders
- Doesn't hint at the "learning" or "strategy deciphering" angle
- Slightly generic — could be any bot competition platform
- "polybot" could be confused with Polygon bots

---

## 2. **whalewars.io**

**Pros:**
- "Whale" is crypto-native terminology (big players)
- "Wars" implies conflict, strategy, winners/losers
- Memorable, slightly aggressive, attention-grabbing
- Implies high stakes and serious competition

**Cons:**
- No connection to Polymarket specifically
- "Wars" might feel too hostile or negative
- Whales typically refers to individual traders, not bots
- Could attract wrong audience expecting whale wallet tracking

---

## 3. **botglass.xyz**

**Pros:**
- "Glass" = transparency, seeing through, looking inside
- Implies you can see what bots are doing (X-ray vision)
- Clean, minimal, modern sound
- .xyz is crypto-native

**Cons:**
- Too subtle — doesn't communicate the competitive angle
- "botglass" is abstract, requires explanation
- Doesn't evoke excitement or entertainment
- No mention of prediction markets or trading

---

## 4. **predictorpvp.com**

**Pros:**
- "PvP" (player vs player) is gaming terminology everyone knows
- "Predictor" ties directly to prediction markets
- Implies head-to-head competition
- Clear that bots are fighting each other

**Cons:**
- Sounds like a gaming site, not finance
- "PvP" might alienate non-gamers
- Doesn't communicate the "watch and learn" value prop
- Slightly childish tone

---

## 5. **marketsharks.live**

**Pros:**
- "Sharks" = smart, aggressive, profitable traders
- ".live" implies real-time action, always happening
- Strong imagery — sharks hunting in the market
- Appeals to aspiration (viewers want to be sharks too)

**Cons:**
- No clear connection to bots or automation
- "Sharks" might imply predatory/negative behavior
- Doesn't mention Polymarket or prediction markets
- Could be any trading platform

---

## 6. **thebotpit.com**

**Pros:**
- "Pit" = trading pit, fight pit, where action happens
- Clear that bots are competing
- Short, punchy, memorable
- Evokes intensity and close combat

**Cons:**
- "Pit" might feel too aggressive or chaotic
- Doesn't communicate the analytical/learning angle
- No mention of predictions or crypto
- Could sound like robot fighting entertainment

---

## 7. **polysnipe.io**

**Pros:**
- "Snipe" = precision, timing, skill (sniping trades)
- Implies these bots are elite, precise operators
- "Poly" connects to Polymarket
- Short, sharp, memorable

**Cons:**
- Sounds like a single bot tool, not a competition platform
- "Snipe" might imply you can use it to snipe trades yourself
- Doesn't communicate the spectator/learning angle
- Could be confused with a trading bot service

---

## 8. **tradecraft.watch**

**Pros:**
- "Tradecraft" = secret techniques, spy terminology, insider knowledge
- ".watch" = spectator, observer, learning from watching
- Implies there are secrets to uncover
- Sophisticated, appeals to serious traders

**Cons:**
- No mention of bots or automation
- Might feel too serious/academic, less exciting
- "Tradecraft" is somewhat niche terminology
- Doesn't communicate the competitive angle

---

## 9. **botleaderboard.com**

**Pros:**
- Extremely clear: this is a leaderboard of bots
- "Leaderboard" implies ranking, competition, winners
- Easy to understand at first glance
- SEO-friendly, descriptive

**Cons:**
- Too literal, lacks creativity or excitement
- Doesn't evoke emotion or aspiration
- Feels like a utility, not a destination
- No hint of the deeper strategy analysis

---

## 10. **alphaduel.com**

**Pros:**
- "Alpha" = edge, excess returns, what traders seek
- "Duel" = one-on-one competition, winner takes all
- Implies viewers can find alpha by watching
- Strong, memorable, aspirational

**Cons:**
- Could be any trading platform
- "Duel" implies two combatants, but we have many bots
- No mention of bots, prediction markets, or crypto
- Might sound like a trading course/guru site

## Top 3 Recommendations

### 1. **polybot-arena.com** (Best Overall)
The original concept holds up well. It balances clarity (you know it's about bots competing) with excitement (arena implies spectacle). The "polybot" compound word is distinctive and memorable.

### 2. **thebotpit.com** (Most Intense)
If you want more aggressive energy, this evokes the intensity of a trading pit or fighting pit. It's punchy and memorable. Drawback: slightly less refined.

### 3. **tradecraft.watch** (Most Sophisticated)
If your audience is more serious/analytical traders who want to *learn*, this communicates that watching leads to understanding. The ".watch" domain is perfect for a spectator platform.

---

## Alternative Approaches

Consider these variations on the winning concept:

- **polymarena.com** — drops "bot", tighter, but loses bot clarity
- **botbattle.live** — emphasizes real-time competition
- **polybots.watch** — spectator angle + clear bot focus
- **predictpit.com** — prediction + pit (trading pit energy)
